How to choose travel insurance without overpaying

The five-minute version of a decision most people either skip entirely or wildly overthink — here's what actually matters.

By Lisa · Ready Jet Roam · Updated July 2026

Travel insurance is one of those things you never think about until the one trip you really needed it. The good news: choosing a policy is far simpler than the comparison sites make it look. You only need to get a few things right.

Start with the coverage that actually matters

Ignore the long feature lists for a moment. For most trips, three numbers decide whether a policy is worth buying: emergency medical, emergency evacuation, and trip cancellation. Everything else is a nice-to-have.

  • Emergency medical — enough to cover a hospital stay abroad, where your home health insurance usually won't follow you.
  • Emergency evacuation — the big one. Getting airlifted or repatriated can cost more than the rest of the trip combined.
  • Trip cancellation — reimburses the non-refundable money you'd lose if you had to cancel for a covered reason.

Match the policy to the trip, not the other way around

A weekend city break and a month across Southeast Asia are not the same insurance problem. For a single big trip with a lot of pre-paid cost, a per-trip policy with solid cancellation cover usually wins.

The bottom line

Pick a policy with real medical and evacuation limits, cancellation cover that matches what you've pre-paid, and a company that actually covers what you plan to do. Buy it when you book, not the night before you fly. Then forget about it — which is exactly the point.
